# Mentora — 2-Minute Pitch Script

Target runtime: 1:55–2:00. Screen-record the app + a small webcam corner for the hook/close if possible (judges rate teams higher when they see a face at open/close).

---

**[0:00–0:15] HOOK — face on camera**

> "Every student has said 'yeah, I get it' right before failing the quiz. The problem isn't effort — it's that neither the student nor most AI tutors can actually tell what you don't know. They just chat with you and hope it sticks. Mentora doesn't guess. It measures."

---

**[0:15–0:35] SETUP — screen share, setup screen**

> "This is Mentora. You paste in whatever you're studying — your class notes, a textbook chapter, anything — and Claude breaks it into a dependency graph of the concepts you actually need to know, in the order you need to learn them."

*(Paste photosynthesis material or click "Try Demo." Show concept graph rendering: 5 nodes with dependency labels.)*

---

**[0:35–1:10] CORE DEMO — the mastery loop**

> "Here's the part that makes Mentora different. Every question you answer feeds a real statistical model called Bayesian Knowledge Tracing — the same technique used in production intelligent tutoring systems. It's not a score. It's a live probability that you've actually learned this concept."

*(Answer a question wrong on camera. Point at the mastery bar dropping / staying low.)*

> "Get it wrong, and Mentora doesn't just show you the same explanation again — it asks the model what you're weakest on, and gets Claude to re-explain that exact concept through an analogy you pick."

*(Click "Cooking" or "Sports" analogy button, show the generated explanation.)*

> "Answer correctly enough times, and your mastery probability crosses the threshold — only then does Mentora unlock the next concept, and only if its prerequisites are already solid."

*(Show mastery bar turning green, next concept auto-recommended.)*

---

**[1:10–1:35] WHY IT'S REAL AI/ML, NOT A WRAPPER**

> "Under the hood, two systems work together. Claude handles the generative side — extracting concepts, writing quizzes, generating analogies. But the thing deciding what you see next is a small, transparent probabilistic model —"

*(Briefly show the Bayes update formula on screen, 2–3 seconds, as a code snippet or overlay.)*

> "— fully unit tested, fully explainable. That's the difference between an AI chatbot and an adaptive tutor."

---

**[1:35–1:50] IMPACT**

> "Mentora works on any subject, any material, with zero backend — a student can drop it on GitHub Pages and start using it today. This is what personalized learning should mean: not more content, but content that responds to evidence of what you actually understand."

---

**[1:50–2:00] CLOSE — face on camera**

> "We built Mentora for the Prometheus July AI Challenge to prove adaptive learning doesn't need a classroom-sized budget — just the right model, paired with the right AI. Thanks for watching."

*(End card: project name, GitHub link, team name.)*

---

## Shot list checklist
- [ ] Record in 1080p, landscape
- [ ] Face-cam for hook (0:00) and close (1:50)
- [ ] Screen recording for 0:15–1:50, cursor visible, no dead air
- [ ] Show a WRONG answer on camera (don't only show correct answers — the analogy remediation is your best differentiator)
- [ ] 2–3 second on-screen callout of the Bayes formula during 1:10–1:35
- [ ] Captions/subtitles burned in (accessibility + judges often watch muted)
- [ ] End card with repo link + team name, visible for at least 3 seconds
